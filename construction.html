Construction

We buy the logs, have them delivered, then get to work. First, we hand peel each log and set them aside. Next, we measure the diameter of each log and select which logs will become the foundation of your new log home.

After the foundation logs are in place on our building pad, each log is notched and laid one on top of the other until the walls of your new home are at the specified height. Then the purlins (roof support logs) are crafted. Purlins are the roof support logs. Handcrafting your log home is a procedure we've done hundreds of times but with each new home we build we use the same care as if it were our own. Once the log shell is done we load it on a truck and it is shipped to you.

Log truck arrives and our crew gets right to work. Logs are taken off the truck in specific order, foundation logs are on top and we work our way down as your walls begin to take shape. The foundation logs are secured in place on your foundation whether it is concrete slab or other construction but of course this part of your construction is done before your log shell arrives. Some people have a daylight basement built beforehand which increases the square footage of your small log home without adding to the cost of your log shell package. Each log is packed with insulation to ensure there is no way for bugs, dirt, or air to get through to the inside of your home. The logs fit snugly together and once caulked, you have a wall which is not only secure but clean and safe. After the walls have reached the height your floor plans indicate, the end pieces of your new log home called, "gable ends", are put in place. Window and door openings are cut. Roof purlins are added and your log shell is complete.

The process moves along at a steady pace and before you know it, your new small log home is ready for the finishing touches. It's fun and exciting to watch your home being created.
